Plot

The tale is about The Little Prince, who visits various planets in space, including Earth.

The book addresses the themes of loneliness, friendship, love and loss. Despite the style of a children's book, its protagonist reflects on life and human nature.

The Little Prince was born on a train. The writer Antoine Saint-Exupéry was travelling on a business trip to Moscow. The train carriages were filled with the families of dismissed Polish miners. Among them he noticed a sleeping boy whose beauty struck the writer. Antoine made a sketch.

It is impossible to imagine a book about the Little Prince without illustrations. Now there are 46 drawings in the book. All of them were drawn by Saint-Exupery.

Antoine de Saint-Exupéry drew sketches of the Little Prince for his friend Léon Vertoux and sent them in letters from the front. At first it was a boy with wings, later the wings became a scarf.

Real-Life Adventure

The acquaintance of the narrator and The Little Prince takes place in Sahara, where the pilot is forced to make a landing because of a faulty engine. It was the true story. In 1936, Exupery and his mechanic miraculously survived and endured for four days without water until they were found by bedouins.

The Plane B-612

Exupery was flying a plane with serial number B-612. The same name of the Little Prince's planet.

In describing the death of the Little Prince, Saint-Exupéry described the death of his brother. He had been ill for a long time, and just before his death he called Antoine to him and tried to comfort him: "I am not in pain. But I can't help myself. It's just my body."

Important Characters

The Fox

The Little Prince's loyal friend, he teaches loyalty and responsibility. The Fox's unusually large ears are inspired by the little desert fox Fennec. Saint-Exupéry tamed him during his service in Morocco.

The Rose

The prototype of Saint-Exupéry's wife. Their relationship was complicated, with adultery and scandals. Rose blossomed on planet B-612, and she constantly nagged the Little Prince and demanded attention. He had to go on a long journey to understand the secret of a happy relationship.

The Baobabs

It's fascism. It's the only thing that threatens the Little Prince's planet. Their seeds infest the soil, and if the sprouts are not weeded out in time, they can grow and tear the planet apart.

"The Little Prince" has become so popular that it has been translated into 300 languages and dialects. More than 140 million copies have been published worldwide. The book is one of the 10 best-selling books.

The French and English versions of The Little Prince were first printed on 6 April 1943. Immediately after the publication of the story, Saint-Exupéry decided to return to the war and crashed in an aeroplane in 1944. In the author's native France, the story was only published posthumously in 1946.
